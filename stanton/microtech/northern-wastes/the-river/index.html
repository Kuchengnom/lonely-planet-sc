The River

MicroTech · Equatorial Tundra

On a planet where surface temperatures plunge to -84°C and unprotected exposure causes hypothermia in minutes, flowing liquid water is a physical impossibility. And yet, cutting through MicroTech’s equatorial tundra in near-perfect silence, the River exists.

No official explanation has been recorded. The leading theory points to a deep geothermal anomaly — MicroTech’s wiki notes that “multiple temperate tundra zones exist in areas associated with deep geothermal activity,” and the River sits squarely in one of them. The dark stone banks are worn smooth, completely free of ice, surrounded by snow that should have claimed this stretch of water long ago. For travellers, that contradiction is the whole point.

Getting There

The River sits in MicroTech’s equatorial zone, roughly 440km from New Babbage. The fastest route takes about 30 seconds once you’re airborne.

From New Babbage:

  1. Launch and set heading to 205°
  2. Engage QT toward MT DataCenter E2Q-NSG-Y
  3. Drop out of QT when you’re ~440km from New Babbage (approx. 900km from the datacenter — press U to exit early)
  4. Descend. Look for a dark patch in the snow below — the river banks are exposed stone, visually distinct from the surrounding white

A small ship is ideal. The terrain is rough for ground vehicles, and the landing zone near the river is tight for anything large.

💡

Stop your QT at 900km from MT DataCenter E2Q-NSG-Y — that puts you almost directly overhead. If you overshoot, the OM-1 / OM-3 triangulation method still works: approach OM-5, then close to OM-3 at 1341.73km, pull away from the planet centre to 1279.97km, then close on OM-1 to 1369.58km.

What You’ll Find

The River is modest by any planetary standard — roughly 40 metres wide at its most accessible point. The banks are dark stone worn smooth by water that has no right to be here. The surrounding terrain is snow-covered tundra with scattered evergreens, typical of MicroTech’s geothermal zones.

There is no loot, no mission, no beacon. It is purely a place to land and stand next to something that defies the planet’s own physics.

That’s the point.
