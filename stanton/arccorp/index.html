ArcCorp

Stanton System · Stanton IV

Urban exploration, cyberpunk vibes, factory tourism, neon photography
Best For

ArcCorp is the most industrialized planet in human space. In eighty years, the ArcCorp corporation terraformed and built on every available surface until there was nothing left to build on — then they built on top of that. Today, the planet is a layer cake of factories, residential blocks, and corporate towers, with the only open spaces being the ones that haven't been leased yet. Area18 is the main port, a landing zone clinging to one of the few patches of ground that can still support a spaceport. The rest is city, stretching to every horizon, glowing with neon and burning with ambition.

Sights & Highlights

1

Area18 — The Last Open Space

Settlement

Area18 is ArcCorp’s main commercial port — which sounds grand until you realize it is one of the only places on the planet where you can still see the sky. The landing zone is a tangle of industrial scars, hab blocks, and the occasional attempt at greenery that lasted until the acid rain killed it. But it is alive. The streets are crowded with workers, traders, and the occasional tourist who thought “most industrialized planet” would be interesting in a fun way.

The ArcCorp Tower dominates everything. It is the corporate headquarters, the tallest structure on a planet that measures success in stories, and a monument to the company that owns the world. The surrounding districts are a mix of factory housing, commercial zones, and the kind of bars that don’t ask questions about your employment history. The Observatory is worth the climb — not for the view, which is mostly more city, but for the scale of the thing. You stand there and try to comprehend that every light you can see is another factory, another home, another life owned by the same corporation.

The food stalls on the lower levels are where the real city lives. Synthetic-protein noodles, nutrient paste “burgers,” and drinks that taste like regret but cost like ambition. It is not good. It is compelling.

2

Cubby Blast

Hidden Find

Every city has a shop that becomes more than a shop. Cubby Blast is Area18’s. Founded by Clor Vee in a back-alley stall of the fourth tier, it grew through the Avasi Scandal of 2931 into a storefront with a favorable lease and a reputation. Vee himself is a character — sharp-eyed, quiet, and supposedly in possession of a memory that never forgets a face or a debt. The shop sells personal firearms and ship weapons, the kind of thing a pilot needs when they are hauling goods through unpatrolled space.

But the real reason to visit is the atmosphere. Vee’s client list includes everyone from corporate security to the kind of freelancers who prefer not to file paperwork. The walls are covered in thank-you notes from customers who claim the gear saved their life, next to news clippings of the ones who didn’t make it. The contrast is the point. This is ArcCorp in miniature: opportunity and risk, side by side, with no one pretending either is safe.

💡

Don’t try to haggle. Vee’s prices are fixed, and his tolerance for negotiation is famously low. If you are looking for something specific, ask directly. He either has it or knows where to get it. The back-alley stalls of the fourth tier are where the real deals happen, but they are also where the real problems start.

3

The ArcCorp Suburbs

Urban Sight

The suburbs of ArcCorp are not suburban in any recognizable sense. They are the spaces between the major industrial zones, filled with the residential blocks and local supplies that keep the factory workers housed and fed. The view from any window is another building. The streets are narrow, the air is thick with industrial residue, and the only green is the occasional company-planted tree that struggles to survive the acid rain.

But there is a strange beauty to it. The neon signs reflect off rain-slicked surfaces in colours that no one planned. The sounds of a thousand factories merge into something like music if you have the right perspective. And at night, the whole planet glows, a continuous carpet of light that has no beginning and no end.

The fusion plants that ring the equator are visible from orbit — a necklace of blue-white energy that powers the world. Tourists are invited to take factory tours, though the actual industrial facilities are too dangerous for visitors. The sanitized versions are complete with holograms and animatronics. The real thing is best viewed from a distance.

4

Lyria — The Cryovolcanic Moon

Natural Wonder

Lyria is ArcCorp’s closest moon, and it is the exact opposite of the planet it orbits. Where ArcCorp is hot, crowded, and entirely artificial, Lyria is cold, empty, and violently natural. The surface is covered in active cryogeysers and cryovolcanoes — eruptions of supercooled methane and nitrogen that freeze on contact with the surface and build into bizarre, transient sculptures. Nothing here is permanent. The landscape changes every time the volcanoes wake up.

The moon has no permanent settlement, which makes it one of the few places in the Stanton system where you can stand and hear nothing but your own breathing. The eruptions are unpredictable but generally safe to observe from a distance — the real danger is the cold, which will overwhelm most suit heaters in minutes. Bring a thermal upgrade. The silence is worth it.

5

The Refinery Districts

Getting Around

The refinery districts are ArcCorp’s beating heart and its most dangerous neighborhoods. Towering processing plants convert raw ore into usable materials 24 hours a day, their exhaust stacks pumping pollutants into an atmosphere that was already struggling. The workers who live here rarely see daylight — their shifts are long, the pay is adequate, and the turnover is constant.

Tourists don’t usually come here. If they did, they would find some of the best food on the planet — small kitchens run by families who have been feeding refinery workers for generations, serving dishes that can only exist here: noodles in synthetic broth, protein blocks grilled until they taste like meat, and drinks that make you forget what the air smells like. The rougher the district, the better the food. That is a universal rule, and ArcCorp is no exception.

6

Baijini Point

Getting Around

Baijini Point is the orbital station above Area18, and in many ways it is the better way to experience the planet. The station has a cargo deck, ship services, and a respectable food court, but the real attraction is the permanent observation deck. From here, ArcCorp looks like a single organism — a vast, glowing entity that breathes smoke and light and never sleeps.

The station’s bar is a favourite of pilots who have seen too much of Area18’s surface and prefer their industry at a distance. The drinks are expensive, the view is free, and the conversation is exactly what you would expect from people who spend their lives ferrying goods to a planet that only stops growing when it runs out of space.

7

Wala — The Prolate Moon

Natural Wonder

Wala is ArcCorp’s second moon, and it is visibly strange. The low density of the moon makes it particularly susceptible to tidal forces, and the result is a noticeably prolate shape — it is wider than it is tall, a squashed ball of rock and ice that looks like it was squeezed by an invisible hand. The surface is unremarkable: cratered, cold, and empty. But the view of ArcCorp from Wala’s surface is one of the best in the system. The planet fills the sky, a carpet of lights and storms that stretch from horizon to horizon, and you realize that everything you are looking at is city. Every light is a person. Every building is a life. And somewhere in there, someone is looking up at you.

Getting There

ArcCorp is a dense, crowded place. Approach Area18 carefully — the airspace is busy and the landing guidance is automated but unforgiving. The planet's surface is one continuous construction project, and the only guaranteed clear approach is through the Area18 corridor. Baijini Point handles overflow traffic. If you are flying a large vessel, expect to be directed to wait. Everyone wants to land on ArcCorp. Not everyone gets to.
