## The Surface

Pyro IV's surface is a study in contrasts. The northern hemisphere is dominated by temperate highlands — rolling plains of iron-rich soil that support a sparse but tenacious ecosystem of lichens and scrub. The southern hemisphere, by contrast, is a wasteland of cracked mud flats and salt pans, baked by the system's red star.

Travel between the two hemispheres is done either by ship or by the ancient rail lines that cross the equator. The rail lines are a relic of a brief period when Pyro IV was seriously considered for colonization. The project was abandoned after the first test settlement was buried in a dust storm, but the rails remain — a monument to frontier optimism.

## Highlights

### The Mud Flats

<AttractionEntry category="natural-wonder">
Vast expanses of dried mud that crack into intricate geometric patterns.

The Mud Flats are Pyro IV's most distinctive feature — a region the size of a small continent where the soil is so rich in iron oxide that it looks rust-red from orbit. When the occasional rain falls, the flats turn into a mirror of liquid copper. When they dry, they crack into geometric patterns that look almost deliberate.

The flats are also treacherous. The surface looks solid but can be a thin crust over thick mud. Several ships have landed only to sink up to their landing gear. The locals mark safe landing zones with signal buoys, but the markers get stolen or moved.
</AttractionEntry>

### The Overlook

<AttractionEntry category="hidden-find">
A natural plateau at the planet's highest point, offering a view across both hemispheres.

The Overlook is a natural plateau at the highest point on Pyro IV, a flat-topped mesa that rises above the surrounding highlands. From its edge, you can see both the temperate north and the arid south — a line drawn across the landscape like a border between two worlds.

The plateau has no official name (The Overlook is what the locals call it), no structures, and no infrastructure. It is just a flat piece of high ground with a spectacular view. And in a system where everything is trying to kill you, a place that offers nothing but a view is a luxury worth seeking out.

*Insider tip: The best time to visit is during the planet's red hour — the brief period each day when the red star is low on the horizon and the entire landscape turns the color of rust. It only lasts for about twenty minutes, but it is the closest thing Pyro has to a reliable attraction.*
</AttractionEntry>

### The Salvage Yards

<AttractionEntry category="settlement">
A sprawling depot of scrap and salvaged ships, Pyro IV's most colorful settlement.

The Salvage Yards are not a single settlement but a patchwork of interconnected scrap heaps, workshops, and black-market trading posts spread across a broad valley in the northern hemisphere. It is said that you can build an entire ship from the parts in the Yards, given enough time, money, and tolerance for things that might explode.

The Yards are run by a loose council of salvage captains and scrap dealers who maintain order through a combination of mutual interest and the understanding that the Yards are only useful if they stay open. Disputes are settled by verbal duels — formal, public arguments that serve as the Yards' primary form of entertainment.

*Insider tip: The best deals at the Yards are not at the main market but at the back row — a series of smaller, less reputable stalls that operate on the edge of the Yards. Here, the dealers sell items that are technically illegal, morally questionable, or both.*
</AttractionEntry>
