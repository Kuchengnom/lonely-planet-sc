## The Belt

The Pyro Belt is a vast ring of rock and ice that stretches from the orbit of Pyro III to just inside the orbit of Pyro IV. It is composed of everything from dust-sized particles to asteroids the size of small cities, and it is in constant motion — a celestial ballet of rock and metal that is as dangerous as it is beautiful.

The Belt is divided into zones, each with its own character and reputation. The Inner Belt, closest to Pyro III, is the most heavily mined and the most dangerous. The Outer Belt, closer to Pyro IV, is quieter but not safer — the asteroids are larger and the gravity wells more complex, making navigation a challenge even for experienced pilots.

## Highlights

### The Maze

<AttractionEntry category="hidden-find">
A dense cluster of asteroids that is a navigational nightmare and a pilot's dream.

The Maze is a region of the Inner Belt where the asteroids are so densely packed that navigation requires more luck than skill. It is a three-dimensional labyrinth of rock, debris, and dead ships that have become part of the landscape. The Maze is a favorite hiding spot for pirates, smugglers, and anyone who would rather not be found.

The Maze is also rich in minerals — the dense clustering of asteroids has created unique geological conditions that have concentrated valuable ores in specific locations. Finding these concentrations is a matter of experience, intuition, and a willingness to risk your ship in a place where a wrong turn means a collision.

*Insider tip: The safest way to navigate the Maze is to follow the ghost trails — faint ion signatures left by previous ships that have passed through. The trails are invisible to the naked eye but show up on short-range sensors. Experienced pilots learn to read them like a map.*
</AttractionEntry>

### The Outpost

<AttractionEntry category="settlement">
An unofficial station carved into a large asteroid, serving as a neutral meeting ground.

The Outpost is a station carved into one of the largest asteroids in the Outer Belt — a massive rock roughly fifty kilometers across that has been hollowed out and fitted with life support, docking bays, and a surprisingly sophisticated communications array. It serves as an unofficial meeting ground for miners, traders, and the occasional pirate who wants to negotiate without weapons drawn.

The Outpost is run by a rotating council of miners and traders who maintain the station in exchange for a cut of all transactions. It is not affiliated with Ruin Station, though they have a mutual understanding: the Outpost stays out of Ruin's business, and Ruin stays out of the Belt.

The station is also a haven for information. Miners talk — about new ore concentrations, about pirated routes, about the movements of UEE patrols. Patience and a few rounds of drinks at the Outpost's bar can yield more intelligence than a month of official reports.
</AttractionEntry>

### The Graveyard

<AttractionEntry category="natural-wonder">
A field of derelict ships that have become part of the Belt's landscape.

The Graveyard is a section of theede Outer Belt where the wreckage of dozens of ships has accumulated. Some are the victims of piracy. Others are miners who pushed their luck too far. A few are ships that simply malfunctioned and were abandoned, their crews rescued or not, their hulls left to drift until they collided with an asteroid and became one with the Belt.

The Graveyard is a somber place. The wrecks are frozen in place, preserved by the cold of space, and in some cases, the bodies of their crews are still inside. Salvage crews work the Graveyard carefully — not just because of the danger, but because there is a general understanding that the dead should not be disturbed.
</AttractionEntry>
