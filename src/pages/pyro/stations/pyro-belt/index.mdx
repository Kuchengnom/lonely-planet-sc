The Pyro Belt is a vast ring of rock and ice stretching from the orbit of Pyro III to just inside the orbit of Pyro IV. It is composed of everything from dust-sized particles to asteroids the size of small cities, and it is in constant motion — a celestial ballet of rock and metal that is as dangerous as it is beautiful. The Belt is divided into zones: the Inner Belt, closest to Pyro III, is the most heavily mined and the most dangerous. The Outer Belt, closer to Pyro IV, is quieter but not safer — the asteroids are larger and the gravity wells more complex.

<AttractionEntry number={1} title="The Maze" category="hidden-find" image="/images/pyro/belt-maze.webp" imageAlt="Dense asteroid cluster forming a navigational maze in the Pyro Belt">

The Maze is a region of the Inner Belt where the asteroids are so densely packed that navigation requires more luck than skill. It is a three-dimensional labyrinth of rock, debris, and dead ships that have become part of the landscape. The Maze is a favorite hiding spot for pirates, smugglers, and anyone who would rather not be found.

The Maze is also rich in minerals — the dense clustering has created unique geological conditions that concentrated valuable ores in specific locations. Finding these concentrations is a matter of experience, intuition, and a willingness to risk your ship. The safest way to navigate is to follow the ghost trails — faint ion signatures left by previous ships — which show up on short-range sensors. Experienced pilots learn to read them like a map.

</AttractionEntry>

<AttractionEntry number={2} title="The Outpost" category="settlement" image="/images/pyro/belt-outpost.webp" imageAlt="The unofficial station carved into a large Outer Belt asteroid">

The Outpost is a station carved into one of the largest asteroids in the Outer Belt — a massive rock roughly fifty kilometers across that has been hollowed out and fitted with life support, docking bays, and a surprisingly sophisticated communications array. It serves as an unofficial meeting ground for miners, traders, and the occasional pirate who wants to negotiate without weapons drawn.

The Outpost is run by a rotating council of miners and traders who maintain the station in exchange for a cut of all transactions. It is not affiliated with Ruin Station, though they have a mutual understanding: the Outpost stays out of Ruin's business, and Ruin stays out of the Belt. The station bar is an intelligence clearinghouse — patience and a few rounds of drinks can yield more useful information than a month of official reports.

</AttractionEntry>

<AttractionEntry number={3} title="The Graveyard" category="natural-wonder" image="/images/pyro/belt-graveyard.webp" imageAlt="Field of derelict ships accumulated in the Outer Belt Graveyard">

The Graveyard is a section of the Outer Belt where the wreckage of dozens of ships has accumulated over decades. Some are the victims of piracy. Others are miners who pushed their luck too far. A few are ships that simply malfunctioned and were abandoned, their crews rescued or not, their hulls left to drift until they became one with the Belt.

The Graveyard is a somber place. The wrecks are frozen in time, preserved by the cold of space, and in some cases the bodies of their crews are still inside. Salvage crews work the Graveyard carefully — not just because of the physical danger, but because there is a general understanding that the dead should not be disturbed without reason.

</AttractionEntry>

<TouristTip>

- **Navigation**: Don't rely on standard charts in the Inner Belt — they're outdated the moment they're published. Use short-range sensors, watch for ion trails, and fly slow enough to react.
- **The Maze**: Only enter the Maze if you know what you're doing, and even then, bring a good reason. It is not a sightseeing destination.
- **Graveyard Protocol**: The informal rule about the Graveyard is to salvage respectfully and leave anything that looks like a crew quarters intact. Violating this rule earns lasting hostility from Belt regulars.
- **Shield Generators**: Not optional. The debris density in the Inner Belt will sand your hull down to nothing over a few passes. Shields are the difference between coming home and being added to the Graveyard.

</TouristTip>
