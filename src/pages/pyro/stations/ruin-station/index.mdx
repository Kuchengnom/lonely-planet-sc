## The Station

Ruin Station is not a single structure but a cluster of interconnected hulks — derelict ships, cargo containers, and purpose-built modules welded together into a sprawling, asymmetrical whole. The station has no unified design philosophy; each section was added by whoever had the resources and the audacity to attach it. The result is a labyrinth of corridors, hubs, and dead ends that even longtime residents get lost in.

## Highlights

### The Grand Market

<AttractionEntry category="settlement">
A sprawling bazaar where everything is for sale and everything is negotiable.

The Grand Market is Ruin Station's beating heart — a multi-level bazaar that occupies the central cargo holds of the Old Hub. Here, you can buy anything from fresh produce to military-grade weapons, provided you have the credits and the courage to haggle with people who don't appreciate being haggled with.

The market is divided into lanes — narrow corridors lined with stalls. The market's most important rule is unwritten but universally understood: no violence within the Grand Market.
</AttractionEntry>

### The Broken Deck

<AttractionEntry category="hidden-find">
A lower-level section structurally compromised but still inhabited.

The Broken Deck was originally a cargo hold that suffered a hull breach decades ago. The emergency seals held, mostly, but the section was depressurized and abandoned. Over time, squatters moved in — people with nowhere else to go, who built airtight habitats inside the wreckage.

The Broken Deck is where the poorest residents live and where the black market operates with minimal interference. It is also dangerous — the hull is unstable, the air is thin, and the residents are not particular about who they rob.

*Insider tip: If you need information that you cannot get through official channels, the Broken Deck is where you go. The residents see everything and forget nothing — provided you can pay their price.*
</AttractionEntry>

### The Lookout

<AttractionEntry category="natural-wonder">
A viewing platform offering an unobstructed panorama of the Pyro system.

The Lookout is an observation deck on the station's highest point — a repurposed sensor array that offers a panoramic view of the Pyro system. On a clear day, you can see Pyro I's volcanic glow, the storms of Pyro VI, and the endless expanse of the system's asteroid belt.

The Lookout is maintained by a small group of volunteers who keep the viewport clean and the atmosphere scrubbers running. The view is spectacular — a reminder of why people come to the frontier in the first place.
</AttractionEntry>
