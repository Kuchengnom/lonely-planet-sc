<AttractionEntry number={1} title="Pyro I — The Hellscape" category="natural-wonder" image="/images/pyro/pyro-1-hero.webp" imageAlt="Molten volcanic surface of Pyro I">

The innermost world is a threat. Molten rock, corrosive atmosphere, and volcanic activity so constant that the surface changes faster than cartographers can map it. Home to some of the most profitable — and most dangerous — mining operations in the frontier. Check your insurance, update your will, and bring a spare EVA suit.

</AttractionEntry>

<AttractionEntry number={2} title="Pyro II — The Glass World" category="natural-wonder" image="/images/pyro/pyro-2-hero.webp" imageAlt="Volcanic glass fields of Pyro II">

Ancient lava flows have frozen into vast Glass Fields of razor-edged obsidian, and a ridge of volcanic spires — the Spine — cuts across the northern hemisphere like a giant's backbone. Pyro I's calmer sibling, but only just. Geologically restless and visually stunning.

</AttractionEntry>

<AttractionEntry number={3} title="Pyro III — The Garden" category="settlement" image="/images/pyro/pyro-3-hero.webp" imageAlt="The irrigated Garden oasis on Pyro III">

By Pyro standards, practically a resort. The atmosphere is thin enough to breathe at low altitude, and the Old Serpent Channel hosts the Garden — the system's closest thing to normal life. Serpent's Rest Outpost is the unofficial capital. The best hand-pressed coffee in Pyro is here.

</AttractionEntry>

<AttractionEntry number={4} title="Pyro IV — The Salvage Yards" category="settlement" image="/images/pyro/pyro-4-hero.webp" imageAlt="The sprawling Salvage Yards on Pyro IV">

The system's middle child. Temperate highlands, arid lowlands, and the Salvage Yards: a sprawling patchwork of scrap, workshops, and black-market trading posts where you can build an entire ship if you have enough time, money, and tolerance for things that might explode.

</AttractionEntry>

<AttractionEntry number={5} title="Pyro V — The Frozen End" category="natural-wonder" image="/images/pyro/pyro-5-hero.webp" imageAlt="Frozen amber methane lakes of Pyro V">

The system's cold, quiet terminus. Most ships stop here only because they have to. The Amber Lakes glow with frozen methane in the star's red light, the Pole Station conducts research nobody else would do, and the Frozen City in the northern hemisphere raises questions nobody has answered.

</AttractionEntry>

<AttractionEntry number={6} title="Pyro VI — The Giant" category="natural-wonder" image="/images/pyro/pyro-6-hero.webp" imageAlt="Pyro VI's ring system lit by the red star">

The outer system's dominant presence — a gas giant wrapped in ammonia storm bands, its Great Scarlet Spot visible from Pyro V on a clear day. Gas mining platforms float in the upper atmosphere, a spectacular ring system surrounds the planet, and everything about it will kill you if you get too close.

</AttractionEntry>

<TouristTip>

- **Ruin Station First**: Always dock at Ruin Station on arrival. It's neutral ground, has fuel and repairs, and the traders there will tell you what's actually happening in the system.
- **Travel Advisory**: The UEE designates Pyro a frontier zone with limited presence. Insurance claims may be denied. Comprehensive weapons coverage and a reliable escape plan are strongly recommended.
- **No Lone Flying**: Single-ship travel in Pyro is statistically poor judgement. Travel with a wingman or stay near established traffic corridors.
- **Credits vs. Cargo**: In Pyro, cargo sometimes speaks louder than UEC. Fuel cells, medical supplies, and food all trade at premium rates. Arrive with something useful.

</TouristTip>
