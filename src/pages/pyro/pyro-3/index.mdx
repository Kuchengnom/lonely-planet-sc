Pyro III's surface is a patchwork of temperate highlands, arid lowlands, and thin strips of vegetation clinging to river valleys. The atmosphere is thin but breathable at lower altitudes, making it one of the few places in Pyro where you can step outside without a full EVA suit. The planet's most striking feature is its network of dry riverbeds — channels carved by ancient water flows that have long since evaporated or been locked underground. The biggest of these, the Old Serpent Channel, stretches for over 3,000 kilometers and connects most of the planet's major outposts.

<AttractionEntry number={1} title="The Garden" category="hidden-find" image="/images/pyro/pyro-3-garden.webp" imageAlt="The irrigated Garden settlement along the Old Serpent Channel on Pyro III">

The Garden is an anomaly in a system that specializes in the hostile. A cluster of settlements along the Old Serpent Channel, it is kept alive by a network of wind-powered pumps and irrigation canals that draw water from deep aquifers. The result is a strip of green that runs for about fifty kilometers — fields of genetically modified crops, orchards of trees that barely belong on this planet, and houses made of local stone with actual windows.

The Garden isn't a single settlement but a loose confederation of farms and workshops, connected by a dirt road that the locals call the Garden Path. Travelers are welcomed with a mixture of suspicion and hospitality — suspicion because Gardeners have learned to be cautious, and hospitality because anyone who makes it this far is probably either desperate or interesting, and either way, they have a story.

</AttractionEntry>

<AttractionEntry number={2} title="Serpent's Rest Outpost" category="settlement" image="/images/pyro/pyro-3-serpents-rest.webp" imageAlt="The walled compound of Serpent's Rest Outpost on Pyro III">

Serpent's Rest is a walled compound of shipping containers, prefab habs, and one surprisingly solid stone building that was here before anyone can remember. It serves as the planet's unofficial capital — a place where miners sell their ore, scavengers offload their finds, and everyone buys overpriced synth-ale.

The outpost is run by a council of merchants who maintain a fragile peace through a combination of bribes, threats, and the occasional public execution. It's not pretty, but it's functional, and by Pyro standards, that's a miracle. The stone building at the center of the compound houses a bar called The Serpent's Head, which serves the best — and only — hand-pressed coffee in Pyro.

</AttractionEntry>

<AttractionEntry number={3} title="The Wastes" category="natural-wonder" image="/images/pyro/pyro-3-wastes.webp" imageAlt="Vast arid plains of the Pyro III Wastes with wind-carved rock formations">

The Wastes are what most of Pyro III looks like when you're not in the Garden. Endless plains of red-brown dust, broken only by wind-carved rock formations and the occasional ruins of a failed settlement. The silence is absolute — no wind at ground level, no wildlife, just the distant howl of the upper atmosphere and the crunch of your boots on ancient riverbed deposits.

Some travelers find the Wastes meditative. Others find them terrifying. Both are valid reactions. The Wastes are also the easiest place on the planet to get lost — the lack of landmarks makes dead reckoning unreliable, and more than a few experienced pilots have missed their ship by fifty meters and walked in circles until their O2 ran out.

</AttractionEntry>

<TouristTip>

- **Altitude and Breathing**: The atmosphere is thin but breathable at outpost level. Any physical exertion — climbing, running, carrying equipment — will leave you gasping faster than expected. Supplemental O2 is recommended for anything more strenuous than a brisk walk.
- **The Coffee**: The Serpent's Head serves real hand-pressed coffee. It is expensive. It is worth it. Do not spill it.
- **Barter**: UEC is accepted everywhere on Pyro III, but barter moves faster. Fuel cells, medical supplies, and quality tools are all excellent currency. Show up with something useful and you'll be welcomed; show up with only credits and you'll be tolerated.
- **Dust Storms**: The Wastes generate dust storms with very little warning. If the horizon starts to go orange, get inside — the particles are fine enough to infiltrate most suit seals.

</TouristTip>
