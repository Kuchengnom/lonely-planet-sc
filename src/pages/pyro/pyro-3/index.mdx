## The Surface

Pyro III's surface is a patchwork of temperate highlands, arid lowlands, and thin strips of vegetation clinging to river valleys. The atmosphere is thin but breathable at lower altitudes, making it one of the few places in Pyro where you can step outside without a full EVA suit. Most outposts are clustered in the equatorial valleys, where the air is thickest and the temperature most stable.

The planet's most striking feature is its network of dry riverbeds — channels carved by ancient water flows that have long since evaporated or been locked underground. These riverbeds are the planet's natural highways, and most ground travel follows their winding paths. The biggest of these, the Old Serpent Channel, stretches for over 3,000 kilometers and connects most of the planet's major outposts.

## Highlights

### The Garden

<AttractionEntry category="hidden-find">
An irrigated settlement oasis that's Pyro's closest thing to normal life.

The Garden is an anomaly in a system that specializes in the hostile. A cluster of settlements along the Old Serpent Channel, it is kept alive by a network of wind-powered pumps and irrigation canals that draw water from deep aquifers. The result is a strip of green that runs for about fifty kilometers — fields of genetically modified crops, orchards of trees that barely belong on this planet, and houses made of local stone with actual windows.

The Garden isn't a single settlement but a loose confederation of farms and workshops, connected by a dirt road that the locals call the Garden Path. Travelers are welcomed with a mixture of suspicion and hospitality — suspicion because Gardeners have learned to be cautious, and hospitality because anyone who makes it this far is probably either desperate or interesting, and either way, they have a story.
</AttractionEntry>

### Serpent's Rest Outpost

<AttractionEntry category="settlement">
Pyro III's most established trading post, built into a bend of the Old Serpent Channel.

Serpent's Rest is a walled compound of shipping containers, prefab habs, and one surprisingly solid stone building that was here before anyone can remember. It serves as the planet's unofficial capital — a place where miners sell their ore, scavengers offload their finds, and everyone buys overpriced synth-ale.

The outpost is run by a council of merchants who maintain a fragile peace through a combination of bribes, threats, and the occasional public execution. It's not pretty, but it's functional, and by Pyro standards, that's a miracle.

*Insider tip: The stone building at the center of the compound houses a bar called The Serpent's Head. It serves the best — and only — hand-pressed coffee in Pyro. The owner, a woman named Iko, claims the beans come from a secret garden on a moon in another system. Given the price, they might as well come from another universe.*
</AttractionEntry>

### The Wastes

<AttractionEntry category="natural-wonder">
Vast arid plains where the wind sculpts the landscape and the silence is absolute.

The Wastes are what most of Pyro III looks like when you're not in the Garden. Endless plains of red-brown dust, broken only by wind-carved rock formations and the occasional ruins of a failed settlement. The silence is absolute — no wind at ground level, no wildlife, just the distant howl of the upper atmosphere and the crunch of your boots on ancient riverbed deposits.

Some travelers find the Wastes meditative. Others find them terrifying. Both are valid reactions.
</AttractionEntry>
