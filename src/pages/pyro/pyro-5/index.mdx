## The Surface

Pyro V's surface is a frozen wasteland — a mix of ice plains, frozen methane lakes, and low mountain ranges that look like they were carved by a sculptor who gave up halfway through. The atmosphere is thin and contains trace amounts of toxic gases, making prolonged exposure dangerous without an EVA suit.

The planet's most striking feature is its network of frozen methane lakes — vast, shallow depressions that fill with liquid methane during the brief (relatively speaking) summer period, and then freeze solid, creating mirror-like surfaces that reflect the sky with eerie clarity. In the light of the system's red star, the lakes appear to glow with a faint amber light, like pools of frozen honey.

## Highlights

### The Amber Lakes

<AttractionEntry category="natural-wonder">
Frozen methane lakes that reflect the red star in shades of amber and gold.

The Amber Lakes are Pyro V's most beautiful feature — a series of frozen methane lakes that stretch across the planet's equatorial region. In the light of the red star, the frozen surfaces glow with a warm, amber light that looks like liquid gold. It is a sight that has made many a jaded pilot catch their breath.

The lakes are also dangerous. The ice is thin in places, and the frozen methane can be unstable. Several ships have landed on what looked like solid ground, only to sink through the ice into freezing liquid below. The locals have learned to test the ice before landing, but the occasional reminder that the planet is not to be trusted keeps everyone cautious.

*InsiderEGO tip: The best views of the Amber Lakes are from the rim of the great equatorial rift — a canyon system that cuts across the planet's midsection. The rift walls are sheer cliffs of ice and rock, and from their edge, the lakes spread out below like a mosaic of amber tiles. The view is spectacular and the landing is... exciting.*
</AttractionEntry>

### The Pole Station

<AttractionEntry category="settlement">
A research outpost at the planet's south pole, studying the unique magnetic anomalies.

The Pole Station is a small research outpost at Pyro V's south magnetic pole, established by an independent research collective to study the planet's unusual magnetic field. The station is modest — a cluster of prefab habs connected by pressurized corridors — but the research it produces is surprisingly important. The magnetic anomalies at the pole have implications for ship navigation throughout the system, and the data collected here is used by pilots and navigators across Pyro.

The station's residents are a mix of scientists, graduate students, and support staff who have chosen to live in one of the system's least hospitable locations. They are generally welcoming to visitors, though they tend to judge you based on how much you know about magnetohydrodynamics. The less you know, the more likely they are to explain it to you at length.

*Insider tip: The station's common room has a small bar that serves what might be the best hot chocolate in the system. The recipe is a closely guarded secret, but it's rumored to involve real cacao, which is worth more per gram than the researchers' salaries.*
</AttractionEntry>

### The Frozen City

<AttractionEntry category="hidden-find">
Abandoned ruins whose origin remains a mystery.

The Frozen City is a collection of ruins located in the planet's northern hemisphere — structures made of an unknown material that have survived exposure to the elements for an unknown length of time. The ruins are not particularly large — perhaps the size of a small town — but their origin is a mystery. They are too old to be human, and their design doesn't match any known alien architecture.

The ruins have attracted a small but dedicated community of researchers, treasure hunters, and conspiracy theorists. Some believe the city predates the system itself. Others think it was built by a lost human colony. Everyone agrees that it is eerie, fascinating, and probably not entirely safe to explore alone.
</AttractionEntry>
