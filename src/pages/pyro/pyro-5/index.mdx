Pyro V's surface is a frozen wasteland — a mix of ice plains, frozen methane lakes, and low mountain ranges that look like they were carved by a sculptor who gave up halfway through. The atmosphere is thin and contains trace amounts of toxic gases, making prolonged exposure dangerous without an EVA suit. The planet's most striking feature is its network of frozen methane lakes — vast, shallow depressions that fill with liquid methane during the brief summer period, then freeze solid, creating mirror-like surfaces that reflect the sky with eerie clarity.

<AttractionEntry number={1} title="The Amber Lakes" category="natural-wonder" image="/images/pyro/pyro-5-amber-lakes.webp" imageAlt="Frozen amber methane lakes glowing in Pyro's red starlight">

The Amber Lakes are Pyro V's most beautiful feature — a series of frozen methane lakes that stretch across the planet's equatorial region. In the light of the red star, the frozen surfaces glow with a warm, amber light that looks like liquid gold. It is a sight that has made many a jaded pilot catch their breath.

The lakes are also dangerous. The ice is thin in places, and the frozen methane can be unstable. Several ships have landed on what looked like solid ground, only to sink through the ice into freezing liquid below. The best views are from the rim of the great equatorial rift — a canyon system that cuts across the planet's midsection — where the lakes spread out below like a mosaic of amber tiles.

</AttractionEntry>

<AttractionEntry number={2} title="The Pole Station" category="settlement" image="/images/pyro/pyro-5-pole-station.webp" imageAlt="The research outpost cluster at Pyro V's south magnetic pole">

The Pole Station is a small research outpost at Pyro V's south magnetic pole, established by an independent research collective to study the planet's unusual magnetic field. The station is modest — a cluster of prefab habs connected by pressurized corridors — but the research it produces is surprisingly important. The magnetic anomalies here have implications for ship navigation throughout the system, and the data collected is used by pilots across Pyro.

The station's residents are scientists, graduate students, and support staff who have chosen to live in one of the system's least hospitable locations. They are generally welcoming to visitors, though they tend to judge you based on how much you know about magnetohydrodynamics. The station's common room has a small bar that serves what might be the best hot chocolate in the system — a closely guarded recipe rumored to involve real cacao.

</AttractionEntry>

<AttractionEntry number={3} title="The Frozen City" category="hidden-find" image="/images/pyro/pyro-5-frozen-city.webp" imageAlt="Mysterious ancient ruins frozen in the northern hemisphere of Pyro V">

The Frozen City is a collection of ruins in the planet's northern hemisphere — structures made of an unknown material that have survived exposure to the elements for an unknown length of time. The ruins are not particularly large — perhaps the size of a small town — but their origin is a mystery. They are too old to be human, and their design doesn't match any known alien architecture.

The ruins have attracted a small but dedicated community of researchers, treasure hunters, and conspiracy theorists. Some believe the city predates the system itself. Others think it was built by a lost human colony. Everyone agrees that it is eerie, fascinating, and probably not entirely safe to explore alone.

</AttractionEntry>

<TouristTip>

- **Ice Landing**: Never land on the methane lakes. They look solid and are not. Use the hardened plains near the Pole Station or the rocky plateaus above the equatorial rift.
- **Thermal Management**: The temperature on Pyro V will find every weak point in your ship's insulation. Landing gear heating is not optional — frozen gear means you're staying until someone comes to get you.
- **Magnetic Interference**: The planetary magnetic anomalies will interfere with standard navigation systems in the polar regions. The Pole Station beacon is your primary nav reference; don't rely on your onboard compass.
- **The Frozen City**: Never explore the ruins alone. The structures are unstable in ways that aren't obvious until something falls on you, and no one will hear you call for help.

</TouristTip>
