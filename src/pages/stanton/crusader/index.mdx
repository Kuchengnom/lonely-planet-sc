<AttractionEntry number={1} title="Orison — City in the Clouds" category="settlement" image="/images/crusader/orison-city.jpeg" imageAlt="The floating platforms of Orison against Crusader's cloud layer">

Orison shouldn't exist. A city built in the upper atmosphere of a gas giant, suspended by nothing more than engineering and optimism. Crusader Industries built it, piece by piece, platform by platform, until it became the most beautiful impossible thing in the Stanton system. The city is a lattice of interconnected platforms — residential, industrial, and commercial — all floating at the edge of the breathable layer, where the air is thin but the views are endless.

The Providence industrial platforms are where the real work happens. Massive transport vessels hang in the construction gantries, surrounded by a web of scaffolding and lights. The scale is impossible to describe. You stand on a viewing platform and watch a ship the size of a small town being assembled in open air, with nothing beneath it but clouds. It is, genuinely, one of the most remarkable things human engineering has ever produced. And it is beautiful in a way that makes you forget how fragile the whole thing is.

The residential platforms are quieter. Cloudrest Retreat and Empyrean Park offer something approaching luxury, if your definition of luxury includes the constant reminder that you are floating over an infinite drop. The sunsets are the best in the system — the light scatters through Crusader's upper atmosphere into colours that don't have names, and the whole city glows.

</AttractionEntry>

<AttractionEntry number={2} title="The Shipyards of Crusader Industries" category="urban-sight" image="/images/crusader/shipyards.jpeg" imageAlt="Massive transport vessels under construction in the Crusader shipyards">

The Crusader Industries shipyards are where the Genesis Starliner and other massive commercial vessels are born. They are also, incidentally, one of the most visually stunning locations in the Stanton system. The shipyards are a web of platforms, gantries, and construction harnesses suspended in mid-air, lit from below by the ambient glow of Crusader's cloud layer and from above by floodlights that never sleep.

The best time to visit is during a shift change, when the platforms are busiest and the lights are most dramatic. But the best view is from a distance — fly a small craft to one of the outer observation platforms and watch the whole thing shimmer in the mist. It looks like a constellation that someone built by hand.

</AttractionEntry>

<AttractionEntry number={3} title="Cellin" category="natural-wonder" image="/images/crusader/cellin-volcanoes.jpeg" imageAlt="Dormant volcanic landscape on Cellin" href="/lonely-planet-sc/stanton/crusader/cellin">

Named after the youngest of three siblings from a 24th-century children's tale, Cellin is as temperamental as its namesake. Over a hundred dormant volcanoes dot the surface, reminders of a planet that is technically asleep but clearly dreaming of fire. The terrain is rough, the geology is unstable, and the whole place feels like it could wake up at any moment. Crusader Industries maintains Security Post Kareah in orbit — supposedly for "research", but everyone knows it is there because the moon attracts exactly the kind of people who need watching.

The surface is a mix of hardened lava flows and ash plains, with the occasional hot spring where miners have set up temporary camps. The view of Crusader from Cellin's surface is worth the landing — the gas giant fills half the sky, its cloud bands swirling in colours that never repeat. Just watch your step. The crust is thin in places.

</AttractionEntry>

<AttractionEntry number={4} title="Daymar" category="natural-wonder" image="/images/crusader/daymar-desert.jpeg" imageAlt="The vast desert of Daymar" href="/lonely-planet-sc/stanton/crusader/daymar">

Daymar is the largest of Crusader's moons and the one that feels most like a place someone might actually want to visit. It is a desert world — not the hot, dry kind, but the cold, atmospheric kind where the dust gets into everything and the sunsets last for hours. The terrain is a mix of canyons, mesas, and vast open flats that look like they belong on a completely different planet. Which, technically, they do.

The surface is littered with the remains of failed mining operations and the occasional outpost that was abandoned when the money ran out. Covalex Hub Gundo was one of the more ambitious attempts — a logistics platform in orbit that someone decided was worth sabotaging. The wreckage is still up there, a reminder that corporate ambition doesn't always end well. On the surface, the real attraction is the silence. Daymar is empty in a way that few places in the Stanton system are. You can walk for hours without seeing another living thing. The dust swirls around your boots and the only sound is the wind.

</AttractionEntry>

<AttractionEntry number={5} title="Yela" category="natural-wonder" image="/images/crusader/yela-ice.jpeg" imageAlt="The frozen surface of Yela with Crusader in the sky" href="/lonely-planet-sc/stanton/crusader/yela">

Yela is the oldest of Crusader's three moons, and it shows. The surface is a water-ice crust that has been there since before anyone named it, pockmarked with craters and split by stress fractures from millions of years of tidal squeezing. It is, in its way, beautiful — a frozen world under a frozen sky, with the gas giant hanging overhead like a vast, glowing wall.

The real attraction is GrimHEX. The Nine Tails — a pirate group with more ambition than sense — carved a landing zone into one of Yela's larger asteroids and have been squatting there ever since. The station is a monument to improvised engineering, held together with welding, hope, and the occasional threat of violence. The locals are what you would expect: opportunists, smugglers, and the occasional honest trader who just wants somewhere to refuel without answering questions. The bar is terrible. The stories are worse. But there is nowhere else in the system quite like it.

<TouristTip>
  GrimHEX is not a place for tourists who want to feel safe. But it is the only place in the Stanton system where you can drink with pirates while watching a gas giant rise over an asteroid field. Bring a weapon you know how to use, don't flash your money, and never — ever — ask anyone what they are shipping.
</TouristTip>

</AttractionEntry>

<AttractionEntry number={6} title="Prospect Point" category="settlement" image="/images/crusader/prospect-point.jpeg" imageAlt="The luxury villas of Prospect Point on Orison">

Prospect Point is Orison's most exclusive address — a collection of luxury villas and observation decks built on a platform that cost more than some small nations. The views are predictably spectacular, the kind of thing people pay fortunes for and then struggle to describe to anyone who hasn't been here. The architecture is what happens when you give a corporate architect an unlimited budget and a directive to "impress."

It is a good place to understand the two Crusaders that exist side by side: the one where people build cities in the sky, and the one where people profit from it. Both are impressive in their way. Neither is comfortable.

</AttractionEntry>

<AttractionEntry number={7} title="Seraphim Station" category="practical" image="/images/crusader/seraphim-station.jpeg" imageAlt="The orbital Seraphim Station above Crusader">

Seraphim Station is the gateway to Crusader, and in its way, it is a destination in itself. The station manages the impossible task of handling traffic to and from a gas giant, and it does so with a kind of bureaucratic efficiency that borders on art. The medical facilities are excellent — word is, the doc here once treated a pilot who had tried to land on the "surface" of Crusader. She survived with a story and a permanent aversion to gas giants.

The real reason to stop here is the view. The station's observation deck looks down on Crusader's cloud tops, and if you time it right, you can watch a shipyard vessel being guided down through the clouds for its first test flight. It looks like a steel whale being born. The station staff will tell you they are used to it. They are lying.

</AttractionEntry>
