<AttractionEntry number={1} title="Covalex Hub Gundo" category="hidden-find" image="/images/daymar/covalex-ruins.webp" imageAlt="The collapsed remains of the Covalex mining hub">

Covalex Hub Gundo was supposed to be Daymar's ticket to relevance — a sprawling extraction facility built by Covalex Freight during one of the company's more optimistic fiscal quarters. The hub processed ore for three years before a catastrophic structural failure (officially: "unexpected geological instability"; unofficially: "someone cut costs on foundational supports") collapsed the main processing tower.

Today, the ruins stretch across a shallow valley like the skeleton of a mechanical whale. The main tower lies on its side, slowly being consumed by dust. Smaller outbuildings remain partially intact, their interiors choked with the fine red powder that defines Daymar. The site has become a pilgrimage destination for urban explorers, salvage operators, and the occasional bounty hunter who knows that abandoned facilities tend to attract the kind of people who don't want to be found.

The real prize for salvage operators is the processing equipment. Covalex cut corners on the building foundations but not on the extraction machinery — much of it is still recoverable, if you can dig it out from under ten tons of collapsed composite. Several entrepreneurial locals have established makeshift camps near the ruins, offering excavation services for a percentage of anything found. Their rates are surprisingly reasonable, considering the alternative is doing the work yourself in a dust storm.

</AttractionEntry>

<AttractionEntry number={2} title="The Shifting Dunes" category="natural-wonder" image="/images/daymar/shifting-dunes.webp" imageAlt="Vast rust-colored dunes stretching to the horizon">

Daymar's most persistent feature is also its most disorienting. The moon's low gravity and thin atmosphere create dust formations that behave like no geology textbook describes. Dunes migrate across the surface at rates that would be alarming on a planetary scale but are merely inconvenient at a human scale — a well-marked landing zone can be buried in a matter of days.

What makes the dunes remarkable is the electrostatic effect. The fine dust particles carry sufficient static charge that, in certain light conditions, the dunes appear to glow with a faint amber luminescence. The effect is subtle — you'll miss it if you're not looking for it — but once observed, it's impossible to fully process. It looks like the moon is breathing.

The largest dune field, known locally as "The Red Sleep," covers an area roughly the size of a small continent. Traversing it by rover is possible but requires careful navigation — the dust is fine enough to clog standard air filters, and the electrostatic charge can interfere with unshielded electronics. Several automated surveying drones have been lost to the Red Sleep, their last transmissions showing perfectly normal readings until they simply... stopped.

</AttractionEntry>

<AttractionEntry number={3} title="The Canyons of Klescher" category="natural-wonder" image="/images/daymar/canyons.webp" imageAlt="A deep canyon on Daymar with layered rock walls">

Named after the security corporation that briefly considered establishing a penal facility here (they abandoned the plan when they realized the escape rate would be approximately zero — not because the facility would be secure, but because there was nowhere to escape *to*), these canyons are among the most dramatic features on any of Crusader's moons.

The canyons cut through basalt formations that date back to Daymar's volcanic youth, revealing strata of rock in hues of rust, ochre, and deep purple. In the right light — during Crusader's "golden hour" when the gas giant's atmosphere catches the sun — the canyon walls glow like stained glass. It's the kind of beauty that makes you forget you're on a dead moon, which is precisely when the dust storm rolls in and reminds you.

The canyon floors are flat enough for ship landings, and several pilots have established semi-permanent camps in the deeper formations. These camps offer nothing in the way of amenities but provide natural shelter from the wind and a view that no luxury hotel in Area18 can match.

</AttractionEntry>

<AttractionEntry number={4} title="The Derelict Flotilla" category="hidden-find" image="/images/daymar/derelict-ships.webp" imageAlt="A cluster of abandoned ships on Daymar's surface">

Tucked into a shallow basin in Daymar's southern hemisphere is something that makes no sense: a cluster of seven ships, ranging in size from a small fighter to a mid-sized frigate, all abandoned in a rough circle formation. They're not crashed — they're parked, as if their owners simply... left.

The ships show no battle damage. No signs of forced entry. Their power cores are cold but intact. The transponders are off, but hull registration tags identify them as belonging to a mining cooperative that dissolved seven years ago. The last known location of the cooperative's fleet was Daymar orbit. No distress calls were ever recorded.

Salvage rights to the flotilla are in legal limbo — the cooperative's assets were liquidated, but the ships themselves were never officially declared abandoned. This has created a bizarre situation where the flotilla is technically owned by a defunct corporation's creditors, none of whom are willing to spend the money to recover vessels that are worth less than the fuel cost of retrieving them.

The result is a ghost fleet on a ghost moon — seven ships with their doors unlocked, their interiors slowly filling with dust, waiting for someone to claim them. Several freelancers have "borrowed" components over the years, but no one has dared fly one out. There's a superstition among Daymar regulars that the ships are waiting for their original crew. Whether that's romantic or terrifying depends on your perspective.

</AttractionEntry>

<AttractionEntry number={5} title="Surviving the Storms" category="practical" image="/images/daymar/dust-storm.webp" imageAlt="A massive dust storm rolling across the Daymar plain">

Daymar's dust storms are low-gravity phenomena — the particles don't hit you so much as envelope you, creating a red-brown fog that reduces visibility to arm's length and seeps into every seal, joint, and filter. A ship caught on the surface during a storm can expect its external sensors to be blinded and its intakes to see flow rates drop by 80% within minutes.

The storms are predictable in the broad sense — they tend to align with Daymar's diurnal heating cycle — but specific timing and intensity are impossible to forecast. The moon's thin atmosphere means there's no weather service, and the dust particles are too small for standard radar to effectively track.

Survival strategy is straightforward: don't be on the surface when the storm hits. If your ship has enough fuel, lift off and wait in orbit. If not, seal everything, turn off non-essential systems to reduce intake draw, and wait. The storms typically last 2-4 hours. The dust is fine enough that it won't damage your hull, but it will clog anything that draws air — engines, life support, cooling systems. Post-storm maintenance is non-negotiable.

</AttractionEntry>

## Where to Dock & Sleep

<WhereToDock title="Shallow Fields Station (Nearby)" category="landing-zone" image="/images/daymar/shallow-fields.webp" imageAlt="A space station in orbit near Daymar">
Not technically on Daymar, but close enough to serve as the de facto clearing house for anyone foolish enough to visit. Shallow Fields is a Crusader Industries fueling depot and transit hub, sitting at the L2 Lagrangian point between Crusader and Daymar. Most pilots refuel here before attempting the Daymar descent, and many linger afterward to swap stories about what they saw in the dust. The facilities are basic but clean — you can get a decent shower, a hot meal, and a secure berth for your ship while you plan your next move.
</WhereToDock>

<WhereToDock title="Derelict Outpost Bunkers" category="habitation" image="/images/daymar/outpost-bunkers.webp" imageAlt="Rugged concrete bunkers built into a Daymar canyon wall">
Several of the abandoned mining outposts around the Covalex ruins have been repurposed by independent operators as safe havens for Daymar explorers. These aren't advertised facilities — you find them by word of mouth or by spotting the well-concealed landing pads. The accommodations range from "barely livable" to "surprisingly comfortable," depending on who's running them. Most offer emergency shelter, basic life support, and a place to recharge your equipment in exchange for a percentage of any ores you extract or salvage you recover.
</WhereToDock>

<WhereToDock title="Storm Shelter Entrepreneurs" category="rest-stop" image="/images/daymar/dust-shelter.webp" imageAlt="A fortified shelter half-buried in Daymar dunes">
A recent development on Daymar is the emergence of storm shelter operators — enterprising types who bury reinforced containers in the dunes, stock them with emergency supplies, and rent limited-access coordinates for a modest fee. The quality varies wildly, but a good storm shelter is literally the difference between life and death if you're caught outside in a Daymar dust storm. Most veteran Daymar travelers recommend signing up with a reputable operator before you arrive.
</WhereToDock>

## Where to Eat & Drink

<EatingDrinking number={1}>
**The Ramen Stand at Covalex Ruins** — Operating out of a converted shipping container near the main excavation site, this unassuming food stall serves the only hot food within 200,000 kilometers. The proprietor is a retired space trucker named Jin, who claims his grandmother's recipe has been through three star systems and two corporate bankruptcies. Food is nutrient-paste based but seasoned with actual spices, a luxury out here. Expect to eat while covered in dust, surrounded by salvagers who measure their life in credits per load.
</EatingDrinking>

<EatingDrinking number={2}>
**Makeshift Cantinas in Canyon Camps** — Several of the semi-permanent camps established in the more stable canyon formations have basic dining setups. Food is dehydrated or ration-bar based, but it's hot and there's usually liquor. The alcohol selection tends toward the industrial — whatever can be manufactured on-site or smuggled in without attracting too much attention. The real value here is intelligence. Canyon cantinas are information clearinghouses — you'll hear about new salvage sites and mining discoveries before they show up on any scanner.
</EatingDrinking>

<EatingDrinking number={3}>
**Shipboard Provisions — Reality Check** — There is no fine dining on Daymar. If you're not eating at one of the dust-covered ground establishments above, you're eating shipboard rations. Plan accordingly. If you plan to spend more than a day on Daymar, bring your own food supplies. The exception is water — several enterprising salvagers have set up water purification rigs that sell recycled drinking water.
</EatingDrinking>

## What to Do

<ActivityEntry title="Mining in the Covalez Ruins" category="mining" image="/images/daymar/covalex-mining.webp" imageAlt="A miner operating a hand-held mining laser on Daymar">
The Covalez Hub Gundo ruins are active mining sites for independent operators with the right equipment. The collapsed processing tower and outbuildings contain recoverable ore pockets that never made it to the refinement stage before the collapse. Hand-held mining lasers and low-gravity extraction equipment are essential. Experienced operators focus on the secondary storage facilities and the main tower rubble, where the highest concentrations of valuable minerals can be found.
</ActivityEntry>

<ActivityEntry title="Flotilla Salvage Operations" category="exploration" image="/images/daymar/flotilla-salvage.webp" imageAlt="A salvage operator examining components from the Derelict Flotilla">
The Derelict Flotilla represents some of the most accessible ship salvage in the Stanton system. Seven vessels ranging from fighters to mid-sized frigates are parked intact, with their systems cold but potentially recoverable. The challenge is speed and precision — the ships are technically listed as owned by defunct corporations. Work quickly, work quietly, and have your escape route plotted.
</ActivityEntry>

<ActivityEntry title="Canyon System Mapping and Survey" category="mission" image="/images/daymar/canyon-mapping.webp" imageAlt="A survey rover mapping canyon walls from a Daymar canyon rim">
The canyon systems of Klescher and surrounding regions contain unmapped cave systems, mineral deposits, and potentially valuable archaeological sites. Several organizations offer bounties for detailed canyon mapping, mineral surveys, and site documentation. Work requires ground-penetrating radar, surveying equipment, and the ability to navigate complex three-dimensional terrain.
</ActivityEntry>

## Getting Around

There is no formal transport network on Daymar. You either fly yourself or hire someone who does. Several independent pilots based at GrimHEX offer surface shuttle services, though their definition of "shuttle" sometimes includes "modified mining barge with questionable life support."

Surface transport is best accomplished with a sturdy, short-field-capable ship. The low gravity means takeoffs are easy — you don't need much thrust to get airborne. The challenge is dust: the electrostatically charged particles will infiltrate engine intakes if you aren't running with filters.

<TouristTip>

- **Be Dust-Ready**: Carry spare air filters. Lots of them. Daymar's dust will clog standard filtration in hours, not days.
- **Electronics Shielding**: The electrostatic charge in the dust can interfere with unshielded electronics. If your nav system starts providing directions in an archaic language, it's probably the dust, not you. Probably.
- **Water Discipline**: There is no surface water on Daymar. None. What looks like a dry riverbed has been dry for tens of thousands of years.
- **Pick Your Landing Zone Carefully**: A flat-looking surface might be hard-packed dust over a boulder, or it might be soft enough to swallow your landing gear. When in doubt, the canyon floors are generally the most stable.
- **The View**: If you survive the dust, the storms, and the loneliness, Daymar offers something no other moon in the system can: the view of Crusader from Daymar's surface. The gas giant fills half the sky, its cloud bands swirling in colors that don't exist in any color palette.

</TouristTip>
