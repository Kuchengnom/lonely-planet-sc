On Daymar’s desolate surface, where the wind-blown regolith stretches to the horizon in endless shades of brown and rust, lies a structure that has no business being there. The Bio-Dome Remnants are what remains of a covert research installation, abandoned years ago under circumstances that remain classified. The intact glass domes, shattered in places but mostly preserved, rise from the dusty plain like fallen cápsulas from another world.

This is not a POI marked on star charts. This is not a mission location with objective markers. This is something pilots found simply by looking where they weren’t supposed to.

## Getting There

The Bio-Dome Remnants sit in Daymar’s southern hemisphere, approximately 800 km from the Simplex Mining Outpost. The OM-triangulation method remains the most reliable cross-patch approach (see sources for multiple corroborated breadcrumb ranges).

**From Daymar Simplex Mining Outpost:**

1. Launch and engage QT toward any OM (e.g., Kareah, or another OM) to break initial fix
2. Drop out when approximately 200–300 km from the Simplex Outpost
3. Re-engage QT toward Daymar Simplex Mining Outpost
4. Watch your OM distances. When they read in the commonly reported triangulation band (e.g., OM-3 ≈ 1172.45 km / OM-1 ≈ 1315.78 km / OM-5 ≈ 1423.91 km; small variations exist) you are near overhead
5. Descend and visually scan. The domes reflect sunlight against the dull surface — look for blue-ish glints

Exactly why these coordinates work varies by patch and OM geometry; Community threads below provide updated ranges and notes.

<TouristTip>
  The domes are most visible when Daymar is in shadow and your ship is illuminated by Crusader’s reflected light. The glass creates a ghostly blue glow that cuts through the darkness. </TouristTip>

## What You’ll Find (Community Reported)

The facility consists of three interconnected bio-domes, each approximately 30 meters in diameter, connected to a central hub by pressurized corridors whose doors have long since failed. Use an extraction cutter or find weak points in corridor seals to enter.

- **Primary Dome — Hydroponics**: Appears to have housed experimental hydroponics. Long-dead plant material, frozen in suspended animation, is visible inside.
- **Secondary Dome — Living Quarters**: Beds, terminals, and personal effects remain; community photos show a coffee mug on a desk and a datapad — a snapshot of daily routine interrupted by evacuation.
- **Central Hub/Third Space — Lab Area**: Equipment has been stripped or destroyed; some community photos and notes suggest lab function, but specifics are unclear.

According to community posts, the central hub still holds some physical notes and items; no official logs or archival materials have been publicly confirmed by CIG or UEE narrative sources.

The structures themselves are remarkable. The domes are constructed from a type of reinforced transparent polymer that remains intact despite the harsh Daymar environment. In direct sunlight, they refract light in patterns that create a kaleidoscopic effect. At night, the starlight passing through them paints the interior in ghostly hues.

## Photographic Opportunities

Lighting conditions and angles create dramatically different moods. Community screenshots and discussions suggest the following approaches:

- **Golden Hour**: Low-angle sunlight through fractured domes creates warm interior glows contrasted with cold exteriors. Use a polarizer to cut reflections and reveal interior detail.
- **Shadow-side**: Position your ship between Crusader’s reflected light and the domes; blue-white illumination against the dark surface yields an abandoned, eerie mood.
- **Aerial Vista**: From ~500 m during the blue hour, the domes form a rough triangular pattern against the moonscape.
- **Interior Shrapnel**: Inside the hub, breached sections show shrapnel patterns on opposite walls — days of rapid decompression and whatever ended operations here.

## Community Context (What It Might Have Been)

The UEE has not released formal records. Community speculation abounds, and two recurring theories appear in posts:

- **Official stance**: Dismissed as an abandoned agricultural research station. Emergency logs are cited as routine decommissioning (reported in forum threads; source links below).
- **Alternative theories**: Some pilots note cratering patterns reminiscent of small-scale bombardment and point to missing facility records. Others discuss references to VULCAN-7 in old data miners’ lists from prior patches. Neither kinetic bombardment nor VULCAN-7’s meaning are confirmed by official sources; they remain community conversation points.

This remains a highly photogenic, mysterious site without an official narrative explanation.

## Safety Considerations (Community Reported)

- **Atmospheric hazards**: Atmosphere around the domes is reported as variable; some pilots note trace compounds that irritate respiratory equipment after long exposure.
- **Structural integrity**: Insulation between polymer layers has degraded in sun-exposed sections; avoid prolonged exposure there.
- **Quantum interference**: Altimeter unreliability within ~200 m and occasional weak local field issues are noted in posts.
- **Encounters**: Some travelers report automated hails and occasional staffed vessels arriving quickly. Official patrol patterns are not publicly documented; treat these as anecdotal reports.
