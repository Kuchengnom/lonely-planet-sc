Find microTech's Rivers and Lake Areas

A practical static guide for locating the known microTech river routes: the original New Babbage river and lake area, plus the canyon river route added later. Keep it open on a second screen, follow the headings, and expect small terrain or marker changes after patches.

Before You Fly

Bring a ship that can hover cleanly in atmosphere and descend slowly. A Pisces, Cutter, 100i, Nomad, or similar small craft is more comfortable than a heavy hauler when you are hunting terrain by eye.

  • Set your respawn and refuel at Port Tressler or New Babbage before you leave.
  • Use daylight if possible. microTech storms and night passes can hide the dry river valleys.
  • Keep a screenshot key ready. These locations are easy to lose once you leave the pilot seat.
  • Remember that patches can shift terrain, marker names, or route accuracy. Treat headings as field bearings, not perfect coordinates.
205

First River: from New Babbage, this bearing takes you into the thawed region near the original river and lake basin.

Canyon River: from MT DataCenter KH3-AAE-L, hold this bearing and watch distance traveled.

Dunboro alternate: short route toward the canyon river area, roughly 60 km out.

How to cancel quantum safely: the RSI community guide says to press U to cut power when the E2Q-NSG-Y target reads about 900 km away. Be ready to regain control, level out, and descend; you may exit quantum at speed or in poor weather.

Route 1: First River and Lake Basin

Fast 30-second method

  1. Spawn or land at New Babbage on microTech.
  2. Point your ship to heading 205.
  3. Quantum travel toward MT DataCenter E2Q-NSG-Y.
  4. Cancel quantum when the remaining distance to the data center is about 900 km.
  5. Look down for a dry patch where the snow breaks. Descend and search the thawed valley floor.

Manual bearing method

  1. Start at New Babbage and climb high enough to clear terrain, ideally several kilometers up.
  2. Hold heading 205 and fly about 440 km.
  3. At the biome transition, reduce speed and scan left and right for water, darker ground, and green-brown terrain.
  4. The lake/basin area is easiest to spot from altitude; the river channel is easier to follow once you are lower.

Route 2: Canyon River

The canyon river is a better sightseeing route than a pure speed run. Give yourself more altitude, and once you are close, let the canyon shape guide you. The channel is easier to follow visually than by instruments.

Primary method

  1. Quantum to MT DataCenter KH3-AAE-L.
  2. Set your ship nose to heading 027.
  3. Fly roughly 440 km while keeping enough altitude to see the valley forms.
  4. When the terrain starts showing deeper cuts and exposed canyon walls, slow down and descend.

Alternate fixes

  1. Use the orbital-marker method: search the planet-facing midpoint between OM-6 and OM-3.
  2. Use the settlement method: start at Dunboro, then fly heading 265 for about 60 km.
  3. If clouds hide the ground, climb until the terrain silhouette reads clearly, then descend along the cut.
What you are looking for: a river channel in a deeper canyon system, with water and occasional lake-like widened sections. If you see only flat snowfields, you are probably too high-latitude, off bearing, or above a storm layer.

Spotting Water on microTech

microTech is mostly cold, mountainous, and snowy, so liquid water stands out by contrast. The most reliable clue is not the water itself; it is the landscape around it.

  • Dry or greener patches: the river areas often sit in thawed terrain surrounded by snow.
  • Long dark channels: from altitude, river beds can look like shadow lines before the water renders clearly.
  • Biome transition: snow giving way to tundra, grass, trees, or darker soil is your cue to slow down.
  • Altitude discipline: search high, confirm low. Too low too early makes you miss the larger shape.
