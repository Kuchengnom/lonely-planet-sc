## Pyro I (Planet)

### 1. The Molten Fields
- **Category**: natural-wonder
- **Location**: Equatorial region, ~40% of surface
- **Description**: An endless expanse of slowly moving lava flows that shift and crack like a living thing. Ranges from slow, viscous flows to fast-moving rivers of liquid rock. Surface temperatures from 700C to 1,200C. Specialized mining suits with active cooling are required for surface work. Short shifts of 15 minutes out, 30 minutes in cooling chamber.
- **Insider Notes**: The fields create rivers of light across the dark surface — spectacular visual effect. Not a place for sightseeing without proper equipment.
- **Status**: Guide complete (pyro/pyro-1/index.mdx)
- **Image**: /images/pyro/pyro-1-lava.webp

### 2. The Sulfur Pits
- **Category**: natural-wonder
- **Location**: Various volcanic depressions across surface
- **Description**: Vast depressions where volcanic gases have condensed on crater walls, creating formations in colors from pale yellow to deep orange. Elemental sulfur deposits mixed with hydrochloric and sulfuric acid in concentrations that dissolve unprotected metal in minutes.
- **Insider Notes**: Among the most profitable mining operations on the planet. Miners wear suits rated for extreme chemical exposure. The pay is higher than average, life expectancy lower. The math, they say, works out.
- **Status**: Guide complete (pyro/pyro-1/index.mdx)
- **Image**: /images/pyro/pyro-1-sulfur.webp

### 3. Independent Mining Claims
- **Category**: settlement
- **Location**: Scattered across surface, between corporate facilities
- **Description**: Hundreds of claims staked by freelancers, desperate prospectors, and criminal enterprises. Not recognized by any government, not on any official map. Property rights enforced at gunpoint. Makeshift prefab shelters, scavenged life support, jury-rigged equipment.
- **Insider Notes**: Do not enter a claim without permission. The claim owner will not ask questions before shooting.
- **Status**: Guide complete (pyro/pyro-1/index.mdx)
- **Image**: /images/pyro/pyro-1-claims.webp

### 4. The Crystal Caves
- **Category**: hidden-find
- **Location**: Deep beneath the surface, away from surface chaos
- **Description**: Vast underground voids lined with crystal formations grown over millennia. Silicon-based crystals ranging from needle-thin filaments to pillars twenty meters across. The formations glow with reflected light from lava flows.
- **Insider Notes**: Mining is done with extreme care — a careless blast can shatter a formation worth more than a spacecraft.
- **Status**: Guide complete (pyro/pyro-1/index.mdx)
- **Image**: /images/pyro/pyro-1-crystal.webp

### 5. Extreme Mining Operations
- **Category**: practical
- **Location**: Major extraction facilities across the planet
- **Description**: Major operations use massive automated machinery operated remotely from orbit. Independents use jury-rigged equipment to extract minerals from areas corporations deemed too dangerous. Targets compounds that form only under extreme volcanic temperatures and pressures.
- **Insider Notes**: Danger pay was invented for people like this.
- **Status**: Guide complete (pyro/pyro-1/index.mdx)
- **Image**: /images/pyro/pyro-1-mining.webp

## Pyro II (Planet)

### 1. The Glass Fields
- **Category**: natural-wonder
- **Location**: Various regions where lava met ancient lakebeds
- **Description**: Vast expanses of volcanic glass — shimmering, razor-sharp obsidian that reflects the system's red star in a thousand directions. Sharp enough to shred most EVA suits. Reflective surface can disorient pilots.
- **Insider Notes**: Breathtaking and lethal in equal measure. The locals laugh at the tourists, but they cash the checks.
- **Status**: Guide complete (pyro/pyro-2/index.mdx)

### 2. The Spine
- **Category**: natural-wonder
- **Location**: Northern hemisphere
- **Description**: A chain of volcanic spires stretching over 4,000 kilometers, ranging from hundreds of meters to over two kilometers in height. Bases honeycombed with lava tubes and caves expanded by miners and smugglers. A key navigational landmark visible from orbit.
- **Insider Notes**: "Follow the Spine to the Heart" — refers to the planet's most active volcanic region at the southern terminus.
- **Status**: Guide complete (pyro/pyro-2/index.mdx)

## Pyro III (Planet)

### 1. The Garden
- **Category**: hidden-find
- **Location**: Along the Old Serpent Channel
- **Description**: An anomaly in a hostile system — a cluster of irrigated settlements kept alive by wind-powered pumps and canals drawing from deep aquifers. A strip of green running ~50km with genetically modified crops, orchards, and stone houses with actual windows.
- **Insider Notes**: Travelers are met with suspicion and hospitality in equal measure.
- **Status**: Guide complete (pyro/pyro-3/index.mdx)

### 2. Serpent's Rest Outpost
- **Category**: settlement
- **Location**: Built into a bend of the Old Serpent Channel
- **Description**: Walled compound of shipping containers, prefab habs, and one surprisingly solid stone building. Serves as the planet's unofficial capital. Run by a council of merchants maintaining peace through bribes, threats, and occasional public execution.
- **Insider Notes**: The Serpent's Head bar in the stone building serves the best — and only — hand-pressed coffee in Pyro. Owner claims beans come from a secret garden on a moon in another system.
- **Status**: Guide complete (pyro/pyro-3/index.mdx)

### 3. The Wastes
- **Category**: natural-wonder
- **Location**: Most of the planet outside the Garden
- **Description**: Endless plains of red-brown dust, broken by wind-carved rock formations and occasional ruins of failed settlements. The silence is absolute — no wind at ground level, no wildlife.
- **Insider Notes**: Some travelers find it meditative; others terrifying. Both valid reactions.
- **Status**: Guide complete (pyro/pyro-3/index.mdx)

## Pyro IV (Planet)

### 1. The Mud Flats
- **Category**: natural-wonder
- **Location**: Southern hemisphere, continent-sized region
- **Description**: A region the size of a small continent where iron-rich soil looks rust-red from orbit. Occasional rain turns the flats into a mirror of liquid copper; drying creates geometric patterns that look almost deliberate. Treacherous — surface can be thin crust over thick mud.
- **Insider Notes**: Safe landing zones marked with signal buoys, but markers get stolen or moved.
- **Status**: Guide complete (pyro/pyro-4/index.mdx)

### 2. The Overlook
- **Category**: hidden-find
- **Location**: Highest point on Pyro IV
- **Description**: A flat-topped mesa offering a view across both hemispheres — temperate north and arid south. No official name, no structures, no infrastructure. Just a flat piece of high ground with a spectacular view.
- **Insider Notes**: Best time to visit is during the planet's "red hour" — about twenty minutes when the red star is low and the landscape turns the color of rust.
- **Status**: Guide complete (pyro/pyro-4/index.mdx)

### 3. The Salvage Yards
- **Category**: settlement
- **Location**: Broad valley in the northern hemisphere
- **Description**: A patchwork of scrap heaps, workshops, and black-market trading posts. Said that you can build an entire ship from the parts. Run by a loose council of salvage captains maintaining order through mutual interest. Disputes settled by verbal duels.
- **Insider Notes**: Best deals at the "back row" — smaller stalls selling technically illegal, morally questionable items.
- **Status**: Guide complete (pyro/pyro-4/index.mdx)

## Pyro V (Planet)

### 1. The Amber Lakes
- **Category**: natural-wonder
- **Location**: Equatorial region
- **Description**: A series of frozen methane lakes stretching across the equatorial region. In the light of the red star, the frozen surfaces glow with a warm, amber light. Mirror-like surfaces reflect the sky with eerie clarity.
- **Insider Notes**: Best views from the rim of the great equatorial rift. The ice is thin in places — several ships have sunk through.
- **Status**: Guide complete (pyro/pyro-5/index.mdx)

### 2. The Pole Station
- **Category**: settlement
- **Location**: South magnetic pole
- **Description**: A small research outpost studying the planet's unusual magnetic field. Modest prefab habs connected by pressurized corridors. The research has implications for ship navigation throughout the system.
- **Insider Notes**: The common room bar serves what might be the best hot chocolate in the system. Recipe rumored to involve real cacao.
- **Status**: Guide complete (pyro/pyro-5/index.mdx)

### 3. The Frozen City
- **Category**: hidden-find
- **Location**: Northern hemisphere
- **Description**: Collection of ruins made of unknown material, surviving exposure for an unknown length of time. Not particularly large (size of a small town), but origin is a mystery — too old to be human, design doesn't match any known alien architecture.
- **Insider Notes**: Attracted a community of researchers, treasure hunters, and conspiracy theorists.
- **Status**: Guide complete (pyro/pyro-5/index.mdx)

## Pyro VI (Gas Giant)

### 1. The Moons of Pyro VI
- **Category**: natural-wonder
- **Location**: Orbiting Pyro VI
- **Description**: Retinue of moons from captured asteroids to small terrestrial bodies. Pyro VIb has a thin atmosphere and a surface of impact craters, site of the abandoned Pyro VI Orbital Observatory.
- **Insider Notes**: The moons are some of the system's most lawless real estate.
- **Status**: Guide complete (pyro/pyro-6/index.mdx)

### 2. Gas Mining Operations
- **Category**: hidden-find
- **Location**: Upper atmosphere of Pyro VI
- **Description**: Massive platforms floating in the upper atmosphere, harvesting hydrogen and helium. Dangerous — storms are frequent and violent, and platforms that drift too deep are crushed by pressure.
- **Insider Notes**: Platform workers have developed a unique culture with slang and "storm stories" — elaborate tales of near-death experiences during violent weather. Best storytellers treated like royalty.
- **Status**: Guide complete (pyro/pyro-6/index.mdx)

### 3. The Rings
- **Category**: natural-wonder
- **Location**: Orbital ring system around Pyro VI
- **Description**: Composed of ice and rock particles from dust to boulder-sized. Stretch for hundreds of thousands of kilometers. In the light of Pyro's red star, they glow with a warm, almost surreal amber hue.
- **Insider Notes**: Dangerous — particles move at high speed. Ships without proper shielding will be sandblasted to scrap.
- **Status**: Guide complete (pyro/pyro-6/index.mdx)

## Ruin Station (Space Station)

### 1. The Grand Market
- **Category**: settlement
- **Location**: Central cargo holds of the Old Hub
- **Description**: Multi-level bazaar where you can buy anything from fresh produce to military-grade weapons. Divided into lanes — narrow corridors lined with stalls. Most important rule: no violence within the Grand Market.
- **Insider Notes**: Disputes are settled outside. The market's enforcers maintain a fragile but functional peace.
- **Status**: Guide complete (pyro/stations/ruin-station/index.mdx)

### 2. The Broken Deck
- **Category**: hidden-find
- **Location**: Lower-level section of the station
- **Description**: Originally a cargo hold that suffered a hull breach decades ago. Emergency seals held (mostly), but the section was depressurized and abandoned. Squatters moved in, building airtight habitats inside the wreckage.
- **Insider Notes**: If you need information you can't get through official channels, the Broken Deck is where you go. The residents see everything and forget nothing — provided you can pay their price.
- **Status**: Guide complete (pyro/stations/ruin-station/index.mdx)

### 3. The Lookout
- **Category**: natural-wonder
- **Location**: Station's highest point
- **Description**: Observation deck on a repurposed sensor array offering a panoramic view of the Pyro system. On a clear day, you can see Pyro I's volcanic glow, the storms of Pyro VI, and the endless expanse of the asteroid belt.
- **Insider Notes**: Maintained by volunteers. They don't charge admission but accept tips.
- **Status**: Guide complete (pyro/stations/ruin-station/index.mdx)

## The Pyro Belt (Asteroid Belt)

### 1. The Maze
- **Category**: hidden-find
- **Location**: Inner Belt region
- **Description**: Region where asteroids are so densely packed that navigation requires more luck than skill. Three-dimensional labyrinth of rock, debris, and dead ships. Favorite hiding spot for pirates and smugglers. Rich in minerals.
- **Insider Notes**: Safest way to navigate is to follow "ghost trails" — faint ion signatures left by previous ships, visible on short-range sensors.
- **Status**: Guide complete (pyro/stations/pyro-belt/index.mdx)

### 2. The Outpost
- **Category**: settlement
- **Location**: Outer Belt, carved into a large asteroid (~50km across)
- **Description**: Station hollowed out and fitted with life support, docking bays, and sophisticated communications array. Unofficial meeting ground for miners, traders, and the occasional pirate. Run by a rotating council.
- **Insider Notes**: A haven for information. Miners talk about ore concentrations, pirated routes, and UEE patrol movements.
- **Status**: Guide complete (pyro/stations/pyro-belt/index.mdx)

### 3. The Graveyard
- **Category**: natural-wonder
- **Location**: Outer Belt
- **Description**: Section where wreckage of dozens of ships has accumulated — victims of piracy, miners who pushed their luck, or simply malfunctioned vessels. Wrecks frozen in place, preserved by the cold of space.
- **Insider Notes**: Salvage crews work carefully — not just because of danger, but because there's a general understanding that the dead should not be disturbed.
- **Status**: Guide complete (pyro/stations/pyro-belt/index.mdx)
