## 1. Aberdeen — Klescher Prison Moon
**Type:** Moon of Hurston (Stanton 1a) | **Category:** Dark Tourism / Hidden Find

### Key Facts
- **Atmosphere:** Sulfur dioxide and sulfur oxide, yellow tint. Trace sodium chloride.
- **Surface Temperature:** 230°C daytime. Lethal without full environmental sealing.
- **Gravity:** Low (like other Hurston moons)
- **Former Ecosystem:** Was the only Hurston moon with thriving grasslands and scattered trees — now dead. Ecosystem collapse likely caused by Hurston Dynamics antimatter weapons testing. Nothing grows.
- **Visibility:** Hazy and cloudy, reduced surface visibility.

### The Klescher Rehabilitation Facility
The facility is Aberdeen's defining feature and only real draw. Operated by the private Klescher corporation, it's an automated prison where inmates work off sentences through a combination of mining labor and time served. The UEE outsources criminal justice to Klescher across the system — clean hands, dirty business.

- Players with **CrimeStat 2+** are transported here on arrest
- Prisoners mine ore under corporate supervision
- **One escape route exists** — requires a Cryptokey to hack a security terminal, then reaching a ship and flying to a security outpost to clear CrimeStat
- The prison's location on a 230°C, atmosphere-toxic moon isn't accidental — the heat is the escape deterrent

### LP Angle
Dark tourism gold. The contrast between Aberdeen's dead grasslands (there *used* to be life here) and the current industrial hellscape of a prison moon is devastating. The implied lore — Hurston tested weapons here and killed its own ecosystem — sits perfectly alongside the brand's wage-slavery contracts and strip-mined planet.

### Suggested Attractions for Guide Page
1. **The Klescher Facility** (hidden-find / practical) — The prison itself. Tours not available. Visiting is inadvisable unless you've broken the law, in which case you have no choice.
2. **The Dead Grasslands** (natural-wonder) — Desiccated remains of Aberdeen's former ecosystem. The last trees died sometime in the last century. Stubs of what were native grasses still poke through the yellow dust. Haunting.
3. **The Toxic Atmosphere Skyline** (natural-wonder) — The yellow sulfur haze creates a light-filtering effect that photographers have called "the Aberdeen Gold." Beautiful from the facility viewing ports. Don't go outside to look.
4. **The Escape Route** (hidden-find) — For the very specific subset of traveler who needs to know this: there is one. Find the security terminal. Get the Cryptokey. Don't ask where.

## 2. Arial — Life/Labor Moon
**Type:** Moon of Hurston (Stanton 1b) | **Category:** Corporate Dystopia / Dark Tourism

### Key Facts
- **Named After:** Arial Hurston, 3rd CEO of Hurston Dynamics
- **Atmosphere:** Primarily nitrogen; trace methane and hydrogen. Fully toxic to humans.
- **Surface Temperature:** Considerably above the boiling point. Heat shimmer makes the surface look liquid.
- **Visibility:** Hazy. Shimmering refraction distorts the horizon.
- **Requirement:** Full environmental survival equipment.

### The Life/Labor Legacy
Arial Hurston (the CEO, not the moon) created the Life/Labor employee contract in the early 26th century — a "years-long agreement at a fixed rate, with little hope of advancement and no option to leave without paying an exorbitant cancellation fee." Critics call it wage slavery. The UEE government continues to work with Hurston Dynamics and considers the contracts legal.

The moon named after her is, fittingly, a place no one can escape from either — the heat and the atmosphere make the surface a death trap without corporate-supplied equipment.

### LP Angle
The moon is a living metaphor. Arial Hurston created contracts that trapped people. Her moon traps you by being physically uninhabitable unless you have equipment that only the corporation provides. The guide should lean into this irony without being too obvious about it — let the reader connect the dots.

No formal outposts listed on Arial — it may be one of the "less developed" Hurston moons. Good for the angle of "the moon named after the architect of wage slavery, itself, has nothing to offer."

### Suggested Attractions
1. **The Heat Shimmer Horizon** (natural-wonder) — From orbit, Arial's shimmer is spectacular. The surface appears to move, as if breathing. It isn't breathing. It's dying.
2. **The Atmosphere Readings** (practical) — A reminder that nitrogen at boiling-point temperatures is still nitrogen. The moon's air is chemically mundane, which makes its lethality feel somehow bureaucratic — like dying to a filing error.
3. **The Naming Legacy** (hidden-find / cultural) — This moon is named after the woman who invented a form of contract that critics compared to slavery. Standing on the surface (briefly, in a sealed suit) while reading her biography is an experience.

## 3. Wala — The Prolate Moon
**Type:** Moon of ArcCorp (Stanton 3b) | **Category:** Natural Wonder / Mining

### Key Facts
- **Shape:** **Noticeably prolate** — elongated rather than spherical, caused by tidal forces from ArcCorp's gravitational pull on Wala's low-density body
- **Atmosphere:** Too thin to breathe, but O₂ can be extracted from the environment by pressure suits
- **Surface:** Barren, but with **outcrops of geode-like minerals** scattered across the landscape
- **Gravity:** Low (implied by low density)

### Surface Locations
- **ArcCorp Mining Area 045** — Surface mining outpost, ArcCorp-owned
- **ArcCorp Mining Area 048** — Surface mining outpost, ArcCorp-owned
- **ArcCorp Mining Area 056** — Surface mining outpost, ArcCorp-owned
- **ArcCorp Mining Area 061** — Surface mining outpost, ArcCorp-owned
- **Lost and Found** — Underground bunker. Unofficial use: drug manufacturing / processing facility. ArcCorp does not comment on its existence.
- **ArcCorp Processing Center 123** — ArcCorp-owned underground bunker. Unclear what's processed there.

### LP Angle
Wala is the ugly little moon no one talks about, shaped wrong by its own planet's gravity, covered in ArcCorp's mining operations, and hiding something suspicious underground. The geodes are genuinely beautiful — pockets of crystalline minerals embedded in an otherwise featureless surface. The contrast between ArcCorp's relentless industrial surface operations and the hidden drug facility is very on-brand for the ArcCorp system.

The prolate shape is visually striking from orbit — Wala looks like an egg, not a sphere. A cracked, heavily mined egg.

### Suggested Attractions
1. **The Geode Fields** (natural-wonder) — Scattered across Wala's barren surface like gifts left by a careless geologist: crystalline mineral formations, glittering in the harsh light. ArcCorp mines around them. Wala doesn't have much, but it has this.
2. **The Prolate View** (natural-wonder) — From ArcCorp's orbit, Wala is visibly non-spherical — it's shaped like a flattened ball pulled too long. Tidal deformation made visible. There's something philosophically appropriate about the universe's most industrialized planet deforming its own moon.
3. **ArcCorp Mining Area 061** (practical) — The most developed of the mining operations. Standard ArcCorp efficiency: everything functional, nothing welcoming. Fuel, repairs, very basic provisions.
4. **Lost and Found** (hidden-find) — The name is the joke. Don't ask what you'll find there. Don't tell anyone where you found it. The people who use this facility professionally prefer discretion, and their definition of "professionally" is flexible.

## 4. Benny Henge — Art Protest in the Yela Belt
**Type:** Installation in Yela's Asteroid Belt | **Category:** Hidden Find / Cultural

### ⚠️ Correction to `reference-hidden-spots.md`
The earlier research listed Benny Henge as being on Daymar. **This is incorrect.** Benny Henge is located in the **Yela asteroid belt**, on a small asteroid near two transmitters. It is not on a moon surface — it's a free-floating installation in space.

### Key Facts
- **Location:** Small asteroid in Yela's asteroid belt (not on any moon surface)
- **Nature:** A Stonehenge-like monument made of **stolen Big Benny's vending machines**
- **Creator:** An artist residing at GrimHEX
- **Purpose:** Protest against "the commercialization of art and the commodification of humanity"
- **Navigation:** **No quantum marker** — must be found via triangulation or following flashing beacons broadcasting the Big Benny's jingle
- **Connected Mission:** "Vending Machine Theft" — machines were stolen from locations across the Crusader system

### The Beacons
Some of the stolen vending machines have flashing beacons attached. These beacons broadcast a looping radio signal — the Big Benny's advertising jingle. Following the sound leads you there. It is both the most and least subtle art installation in the known systems.

### LP Angle
This is the kind of thing Lonely Planet exists to document. An anonymous artist at the system's most notorious outlaw station steals fast food vending machines from across the Crusader system, hauls them into space, and arranges them in a Stonehenge formation as a protest against commercialization. Then broadcasts the corporation's own jingle on a loop to mark the spot.

It's brilliant. It's absurd. It's in the middle of an asteroid field. It has no formal address. It's exactly right.

### Suggested Attractions
1. **Benny Henge** (hidden-find) — A circle of decommissioned Big Benny's vending machines arranged in the style of the ancient Stonehenge monument on Earth. Each machine still has its branded panel. In the right light, with Crusader visible behind them, the effect is genuinely moving — which is either a testament to the artist's vision or evidence that you've been alone in space too long.
2. **The Radio Beacon Hunt** (practical / hidden-find) — There is no marker on your map. The only guide is the faint signal broadcast by the machines themselves. Follow the jingle. It will lead you somewhere unexpected, which is the point.
3. **GrimHEX Connection** (context) — The artist lives at GrimHEX. This may explain how they managed to steal this many vending machines without getting arrested — or perhaps the right people at the right outlaw station simply didn't care. Either way, the provenance adds to the legend.

## 5. Additional Research Leads (Not Yet Developed)

### Magda (Hurston moon, Stanton 1c)
- Named after Magda Hurston, the CEO who purchased Stanton I from the UEE
- Minimal wiki data — surface conditions likely similar to other Hurston moons (hostile)
- LP angle: the moon named after the person who *bought the planet* — the original sin of the Stanton corporate era

### Ita (Hurston moon, Stanton 1d)
- Named after Ita Hurston, Chief Warrant Officer, died in the First Tevarin War (2541–2546)
- Atmosphere: mostly nitrogen, trace methane and carbon monoxide
- LP angle: the only Hurston moon named for a loss rather than a legacy. Hurston Dynamics uses her death to justify the weapons it builds — "a reminder of why Hurston's products are important."

### Aaron Halo (Asteroid Belt, between Crusader and ArcCorp)
- Primary asteroid belt of the Stanton system
- Major mining location with Rest Stop stations at Lagrangian points
- Could become a standalone guide or a section of the Crusader/ArcCorp pages
